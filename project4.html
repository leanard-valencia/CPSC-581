FeelSense: AR Emotion Guide

For the last project, we aim to create a novel interface to augment in-person and/or remote communication and collaboration. We present FeelSense: AR Emotion Guide, a groundbreaking tool designed to empower individuals with autism in understanding and navigating social interactions more confidently. By harnessing augmented reality technology, this app offers real-time emotion recognition. Users simply point their phone towards the person they're engaging with, and the app seamlessly scans and identifies the emotions being expressed. For individuals on the autism spectrum, interpreting emotions can often be challenging, but our app bridges that gap by providing clear and concise guidance on how to respond appropriately to each identified emotion. This innovative solution aims to enhance social communication skills, foster empathy, and promote more meaningful connections, ultimately fostering a more inclusive and understanding society.

Initial Sketches

For our initial ideation process, each group member independently produced 10 design sketches centred on proposed applications that foster communication and collaboration. Each member was given freedom to sketch any idea or topic in order to produce a wider range of options for the project idea.

Virtual Brainstorming Room (John)

An immersive VR environment where teams can collaborate, ideate, and brainstorm remotely. This will allow users to interact in a virtual whiteboard where they can sketch and add notes simultaneously.

Live Sign Language Translation (John)

A software that will seamlessly translate sign language. This aims to bridge the gap in communication for deaf and hearing problems so that they can participate in communication real-time.

More than words (John)

The idea takes inspiration from friendship (two-way) lamps. The users will assign a colour to certain emotions or messages. This will allow users to easily communicate their feelings just by displaying a colour on the receiver.

Collaborative Photo Journal (John)

This application aims to encourage friends/families to collaborate in compiling special items that will help them keep their memories. This application aims to provide more personalization in sharing memories.

Multiplayer Gaming Overlay (Bassel)

The overlay seamlessly integrates with various gaming platforms, fostering collaborative gameplay and engaging interactions among players.

Collaborative Roleplaying (Bassel)

Participants collaboratively craft the storyline, shape characters, and influence the unfolding plot, fostering a dynamic and engaging storytelling experience.

Collaborative Sketchbook (Bassel)

Collaborative Sketchbook is an interactive platform that allows multiple artists to contribute and build upon each other's sketches in a digital space, fostering a communal canvas for creative expression and artistic collaboration.

Holographic Meetings (Mahian)

This innovative platform merges augmented reality with conferencing, offering a seamless, interactive, and visually captivating environment for meetings and discussions.

Emotion AR (Mahian)

The app offers a seamless blend of AR and emotional intelligence, providing users with nuanced insights into the emotional states of those around them. This app is designed to enhance social interactions and empathetic communication through visual cues and prompts.

AR Training (Mahian)

Users engage in lifelike simulations, enhancing comprehension and retention through immersive, practical application within their work environment.

Refined Sketches

Next, we again independently created refined sketches based on our initial sketches. Each of us sketched polished iterations and different approaches of the initial ideas,culminating in the selection of our final idea for further development. The top three(3) ideas that resonated with us the most are: Live Sign Language Translation, More Than Words, and Emotion AR.

More Than Words Lamp (John)

A refinement of the initial “More Than Words” idea, which integrates the lamp feature more literally. Users can get the experience of tapping the lamp in order to communicate feelings to the receiver.

Group Journals (John)

A refinement of the initial journaling idea.This introduces a feature to create and manage different journals for different groups where each group can modify the journals they belong in.

AI Collaborative Sketchbook (John)

Incorporated AI into the collaborative sketchbook where in the software can generate suggestions and prompts for different components of the drawing.

Refined Collaborative Whiteboard (John)

Integrated chats and “To-do” lists section to the whiteboard to make it more informative for planning and collaboration.

More Than Words - Phone Background (Bassel)

A refinement of the “More Than Words” idea that seamlessly changes the phone background to the chosen colour to communicate feelings with one another. This refinement aims for easier access and optimised navigation of colour picking and sending.

Live Sign Language Translation (Bassel)

Introduction of ear implants as a discreet device that will be useful for a faster and seamless translation delivery to the users.

AR 3D Interactive Whiteboard (Bassel)

By transforming the initial AR Whiteboard into 3D space, it enables users to visualise, annotate, and manipulate 3D content in real-time, fostering more real-life experience.

AR Automated Thank You Letter (Mahian)

A combination of VR Gift Giving idea and the AR Greeting cards. This aims to provide an immersive experience on both the giver and receiver.

AI Live Sign Language Translator (Mahian)

Incorporates AI technology to Live Sign Language Translator to utilise predictive translation in order to provide a faster transcription to the users.

AI Assistant for AR WarGaming (Mahian)

A refinement of collaborative wargaming which uses AI to provide suggestive game strategy to the player through text outputs in the AR environment.

The Problem

Individuals with autism often struggle to recognize emotions in others, hindering their social interactions and understanding. There's a need for support in interpreting and comprehending various emotions for improved social engagement and appropriate response.

The Solution

FeelSense: AR Emotion Guide employs augmented reality cues, aiding individuals with autism in interpreting emotions during social interactions, fostering greater understanding and confidence. Additionally, it offers guidance on appropriate responses, empowering users with insights on how to navigate and respond effectively to various emotional cues in social settings.

Final Design and Contributions

Upon evaluating our initial sketches and refined sketches, we narrowed down our options to two compelling choices: the AR Emotion Guide by Mahian and Live Sign Language translation by me. Both ideas held immense promise, offering distinct advantages to address specific societal needs. After careful consideration, we gravitated towards the AR Emotion Guide by Mahian due to its unparalleled potential to delve into the intricacies of human emotion, a realm crucial in understanding and enhancing human interaction, empathy, and psychological well-being. Beyond verbal communication, we acknowledged the significant role emotions play in communication, leading us to envision the FeelSense: AR Emotion Guide as practical support for individuals with autism, empowering them with enhanced guidance for more effective social interactions. The implementation of this idea was done in Glitch by Bassel. Bassel utilised Teachable Machines for emotion recognition and then used emoji output for emotion counterpart and text output for suggestive response on unique situations. I created the Rotoscope sketch for the project idea and documented the ideation and implementation process for the writeup. Mahian created the concept video and the demo presentation for the project.
