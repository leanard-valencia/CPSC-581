Jungle Talk

The objective of this project was to create an interactive application geared towards children. Our group decided to design a therapeutic and educational game for children with down syndrome, autism, and other disabilities that affect speech. Our aim was to aid the development of speech in these children as well as to give them a sense of achievement and fun.

Initial Sketches

Our first task was to create 10 sketches per person, each representing a different idea we might want to explore. We hadn’t agreed on a focus for our sketches beforehand, and instead decided to come up with as many unique ideas as possible.

Speech Therapy (Bassel)

An application geared towards parents of children with down syndrome or other speech development issues, to be used as a tool to assist in education.

Alphabet Animals (John)

An educational game intended to teach children the names and spellings of different animals.

Celestial Drawing (Mahian)

A game where children would connect the stars to draw real constellations.

My Digital Diary (Bassel)

A digital diary for children to write and draw in with customisable fonts, page designs and diary covers.

Personalized Story Telling (John)

A personalized storytelling app where children would be able to create their own stories based on pre-created modules

Trash Sorter (Mahian)

An educational game which would teach children how to properly sort trash into trash, recycling and compost, and reward them for doing so.

Hidden Shapes (John)

An educational game where children would spot hidden shapes in images, and learn about them when they are found.

Kid's Weather (John)

An educational application that would allow children to select and learn about different seasons and weather conditions.

Digital Musical Instruments (Mahian)

A series of virtual instruments which children would be able to interact with to make music.

Model Solar System (Bassel)

An educational interactive model of Earth's solar system, which children would be able to manipulate to learn about space and planets.

Refined Sketches

Our next task was to create 10 more sketches per person, where each sketch was to be a refinement on one of the previous sketches. We once again hadn’t agreed on a focus for our sketch refinements, to avoid impeding creativity. 3 ideas saw the most attention: Speech Therapy, Digital Musical Instruments, and Personalized Story Telling.

Animated Pronunciation Buddy (John)

Refinement of "Speech Therapy" sketch - replaces the animated mouth with a mascot to shift the target audience from parents to children.

Speech Quest (John)

Refinement of "Speech Therapy" sketch - gamification of speech therapy to shift the target audience from parents to children.

Accents/Dialects (Bassel)

Refinement of "Speech Therapy" sketch - provides different accents/dialectical pronunciations for different parts of the world.

Speech Analysis (Mahian)

Refinement of "Speech Therapy" sketch - allows children to record input and provides pronunciation feedback.

Modular Text (Bassel)

Refinement of "Personalized Story Telling" sketch - allows children to customize a story with randomly selected options where each prompt is based entirely on previous selection.

Modular Images (Bassel)

Refinement of "Personalized Story Telling" sketch - allows children to customize a story with randomly selected options in the form of images depicting the story advancements.

Doodle Music (John)

Refinement of "Digital Musical Instruments" sketch - allows children to draw and then play music using the drawings, by associating each drawing with a sound.

Musical Sound Sequence (John)

Refinement of "Digital Musical Instruments" sketch - a game where children learn music by repeating sequences played by the app.

Musical Spelling (John)

Refinement of "Digital Musical Instruments" sketch - a game where children learn to spell and enjoy music by associating letters in a word with sounds.

Connect the Stars (Bassel)

Refinement of "Celestial Drawing" sketch - replaces real constellations with cartoon drawings to interest children more.

Final Design and Contributions

For our final design, we decided to implement the speech therapy application, as we felt it had the most potential with its specific target audience. It seemed to be the idea that resonated with each of us the most. Although Bassel's original idea was targeted towards parents of children with speech disabilities, I improved upon the idea by changing the target audience to the children themselves with my “Speech Quest” and “Animated Pronunciation Buddy” sketches. Building upon this idea, Mahian suggested that we should combine the application with my “Animal Alphabets” sketch. For our final application, we had 3 layers of pages. The 1st layer consists of 1 page which has a series of animated animals as clickable buttons. Each of these buttons lead to a page in the 2nd layer. This layer consists of 7 pages - one for each animal - and each contains the spelling of the particular animal, a button which pronounces it, and a video which acts as a detailed pronunciation guide. Late into our development cycle, we added a 3rd layer, accessible from within the 2nd layer. This layer implements Mahian's speech analysis sketch to provide feedback to users, enhancing the learning experience. The application was implemented in glitch by Mahian and I. The write-up was done by Bassel and the video was done by me.
